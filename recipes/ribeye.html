The Flatwoods Ribeye

A good ribeye steak is a luxury in any world. Whether you’re scraping by in a post‑nuclear Appalachia or just firing up the grill in your backyard, a thick-cut ribeye is still a promise of good eating. In the wasteland, you might sear it over a rusted mesh grate balanced on a trashcan fire. At home, you’ve probably got a stovetop that doesn’t require duct tape to stay level. Either way, the goal’s the same: a tender, smoky slab of meat that reminds you life can still taste damn good. So let’s cook one up—no radstorms, raiders, or deathclaws required.

Ingredients

  • Ribeye Steak - 12 to 16 Oz Cut
  • Seasonings - Course salt and freshly ground black pepper and garlic powder
  • Oil - A high smoke-point oil like avocado, canola, or vegetable oil.
  • Aromatics - A shallot, garlic cloves and fresh thyme.
  • Butter - Baste the steak in melted butter

Steps

  1. Season The Steak - Pat the steak dry with pepper towels and season liberally on both sides with the salt, pepper, and garlic.
  2. Head The Pan - Head a thing layer of oil in a large, thick-bottomed skillet over high heat until it starts to shimmer and lightly smoke.
  3. Sear The Steak - Carefully place the steak into the hot pan, reduce the heat slightly, and DO NOT TOUCH for a full minute. This allows a crust to form.
  4. Move And Flip - Gently move the steak freely around the pan for another full minute. Flip it to the other side and repeat the seat and the moving.
